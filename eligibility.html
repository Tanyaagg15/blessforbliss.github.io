Eligibility Criteria for Adoption

Who can adopt in India?

The following are the basic conditions which need to be satisfied by the adopting parents in order to be eligible to adopt a child:

  • A child or parent in India can be adopted by an Indian citizen, NRI or a foreign citizen. However, the procedure of adoption is different for all three.Any person is eligible to adopt irrespective of age, gender etc.
  • If a couple is adopting a child, they should have completed at least two years of stable marriage and have a joint consensus for adopting the child.
  • The adoptive parents must be at least 25 years old and not more than 55 years old.
  • The adoptive parents or children must be physically, mentally, emotionally stable, financially capable of fulfilling all the needs of adopted child or parent.

When a child/parent is eligibile to adopt?

  • As per the guidelines of the Central Government of India, any orphan, abandoned (desserted or unaccompanied by the legal parents or children) or surrendered (has been relinquished because of physical, social and emotional factors beyond the control of the family) child declared legally free for adoption by the child welfare committee is eligible for adoption.
  • In order to be adopted, a child or parent needs to be “legally free”. On receipt of an abandoned child/parent, the District Child Protection Unit puts up an alert with the child’s/parent's photograph and details in state-wide newspapers and requests the local police to trace the parents or children. The child or parent is considered legally free for adoption only after the police have given a report stating that the parents of the child or the children of the parents are non-traceable.

What are the Normal Conditions to be Fulfilled by Parents/Children?

CARA has defined the eligibility criteria for prospective adoptive parents in order to be able to adopt a child. They are as follows:

  • The prospective adoptive parents or children need to be physically, emotionally, financially and mentally stable.
  • The prospective parents or children should not be suffering from any life-threatening diseases.
  • Couples with three or more kids are not considered for adoption except in the case of special-needs children.
  • The parents’ age as of the date of registration should be as per CARA guidelines to be eligible for adoption.
  • Parents/Children must provide a safe and secure home environment for their children or parents. This includes protecting the child/parent from physical harm, neglect, and abuse
  • Parents/Children must meet their children’s/parents' basic needs, including food, clothing, shelter, and healthcare. They must also ensure their child’s educational and developmental needs are met.
  • Effective communication is key to building a positive parent-child relationship. Parents/Children should listen carefully to their children/parents, respect their opinions, and provide honest and accurate information.

Documents Required for adoption

  • Present photograph of family or parent who is adopting the child.
  • Aadhar Card or Passport or Voter card or Driving License of the prospective adoptive parents or Birth Certificate or Overseas Citizen of India card of the prospective adoptive parents (if applicable) and PAN Card (if applicable).
  • Proof of residence (Aadhar card/ voter card/ passport/ current electricity bill/ telephone bill)
  • Proof of income of last year (salary slip/ income certificate issued by Govt. department/ income tax return)
  • Certificate from a medical practitioner certifying that the prospective adoptive parents do not suffer from any chronic, contagious or fatal disease and they are fit to adopt (In the event of a married couple, upload the applicant’s respective medical certificate)
  • Marriage certificate/Divorce Decree/Declaration from the competent court or affidavit on oath pertaining to divorce in case of divorce governed by personal law where decree of divorce is not mandatory/Death certificate of spouse whichever is applicable.
  • Undertaking from the relative in case single prospective adoption parents (if applicable).
  • Consent of the older child/children in the adoptive family.
